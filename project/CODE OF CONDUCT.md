# Code of Conduct — The Drake Display

## Our commitment

This project is a place to think rigorously and build honestly, together. We want it to be welcoming to astrophysicists, engineers, writers, and the merely curious alike — regardless of background, identity, or experience level.

## The standard we hold

Beyond the usual (and expected) baseline of treating people with respect, this project has one norm that defines its culture:

**Intellectual honesty and good-faith disagreement are the whole point.**

- Challenge ideas vigorously; treat people generously. The two are not in tension.
- "Here is my uncertainty, and here is my reasoning" is always welcome. Confident assertion without evidence is not.
- Being corrected is not a loss of face — it is the mechanism by which the model gets better. Correct others kindly; accept correction gracefully.
- Cite your sources. Do not fabricate authority.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and welcoming.
- Assume good faith; ask before assuming bad intent.
- Keep criticism about the work, not the person.
- Help newcomers find their footing across all three tracks (science, model, build).

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Dismissiveness toward good-faith questions or toward contributors' backgrounds or skill levels.
- Deliberately misrepresenting evidence, fabricating citations, or presenting speculation as established fact after being asked to flag it.
- Sustained disruption of discussion.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — the repository, Discussions, issues, pull requests, and any associated channels — and to public spaces when representing the project.

## Enforcement

Concerns may be reported to the project maintainer at <drake-project@hecarrieswater.com>. Reports will be handled discreetly and in good faith. The maintainer may warn, temporarily restrict, or remove participants whose conduct harms the community, at their reasonable discretion.

## Spirit

Rigor without cruelty; honesty without ego; welcome without lowering the bar. If in doubt, err toward kindness and toward showing your reasoning.
